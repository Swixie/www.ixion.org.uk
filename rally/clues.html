Ixion Rally - Guidelines for Clue-Givers

The cost of entry to the Ixion Rally is that you provide one or more clues, and mark people's answers to your clues. We distinguish "Rally" clues, and "Treasure-hunt" clues. More points will be awarded for the latter, cos they're harder.

All that's required is one rally-style clue, but if you feel inclined, and there's enough of interest round your way, feel free to submit a couple. Here are some guidelines for your clues:

Rally Clues

Rally clues are just to prove that you've been to the place, e.g. "what's the colour of the clock face on the church?

  • Must be within 10 minutes ride[1] of your house
  • Must give a placename that multimap or streetmap understands - e.g. not Sutton, but Sutton, Doncaster.
    (When you submit your clue, you'll be asked for a streetmap URL, and you can assume that the solver has seen it)
  • You must be able to take an acceptable photograph of your bike in the place from the road (car parks are dodgy if they're closed, or used for markets)

Treasure-Hunt Clues

Treasure-Hunt clues are slightly trickier, and should need a bit of thinking - not exactly MENSA-level stuff, but harder than just looking at a clock to see what colour it is. I STFW and found some treasure hunt questions, these show the kind of thing:

  • Who was the High Flyer who died for the sake of his health?
  • Which house has the wrong colour rose and out of date venison?
  • On which house wouldn't the sun shine?
  • Our sleeping policeman wants information on Dr Who's enemy. What number should you ring?

Of course, these need the solver to be in the vicinity first: to get them there can be part of the clue, or you can just tell 'em.

More about the clues:

  • Must be within 20 minutes ride[1] of your house
  • Might require up to 10 minutes off-road riding, but must mention that, should be suitable for e,g, R1150GS or Tiger, and of course it must be legal :->
  • Might require walking, but only up to 10 minutes (for the ISO standard Ixi-in-full-kit)

[1] where riding time is measured at "normal pace" in sunny-summer-Sunday-afternoon road and traffic conditions
