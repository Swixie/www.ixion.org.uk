Jeremy's Question-Setting Guide

Glorious winner of IxiRally'03 offers this advice for the setting of questions ...

  • Make something about the question appealing, be it a clever clue, an interesting place to go or good riding to get there. Questions that have none of these things tend to be very tedious. Two out of the three would be a good thing to aim for.
  • Choose questions where parking for several bikes is easy and safe.
  • Consider if the place will be there/open for the duration of the rally, and that rallyists may be answering the question at night or in the dark.
  • Think about how close the answer can be approached - getting good photos of distant objects is tricky, and nigh on impossible in the dark.
  • Don't deliberately mis-mark the location of the answer. If you want to make it harder be clever with the clue.
  • Ixies are a bunch of geeky internerds so make some of your question answerable from web searches (they still have to go there to take a photo).
  • Ixies are a bunch of geeky internerds so make some of your questions unanswerable from web searches (they have to go there and search around (briefly, not for hours) to find the answer).
  • Don't set questions in towns/cities unless the clue/answer is particularly clever/interesting. There is no good riding to be had in towns, and it is unlikely any Ixies are going to find your local or place of work worth visiting.
  • Don't set questions in towns/cities. It's worth mentioning twice ;-)
