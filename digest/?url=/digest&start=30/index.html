brake pad shims

Posted by Ric Davis <ricdavis-at-gmail.com> on 14 June 2014 | 0 Comments

I've just had the joy of replacing the rear pads in my Fazer
(underslung caliper, with no drainage and threaded pad securing pins
right at the bottom - I'm toying with drilling a drain hole).

The pads have shims that clip to the back, which isn't so unusual.
What seems odd is that the shims are half width and go at the front of
the pads, which because it is an underslung caliper, is the pad
leading edge.

Normally, pads wear fastest at the leading edge, so when you replace
them, they're somewhat wedge shaped.

Why would you design the shims to make this tapered wear worse? I'd
have thought you'd want them on the trailing edge to give more even
wear.

--
Ric
