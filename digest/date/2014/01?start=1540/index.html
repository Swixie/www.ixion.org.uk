Re: Breakdown recovery

Posted by andy pugh <bodgesoc-at-gmail.com> on 1 January 2014 | 0 Comments

> From calling them to arriving at a garage a mile away, three hours. Took
> less time for the garage to do the repair.

Had some car-related fun this evening.

A Yoof attending a new-year party in the village managed to snag a
sturdy post reversing into the village. (You need to reverse in if you
arrive from the uphill direction).
http://goo.gl/maps/0SAOZ

Unfortunately he managed to break his steering rack. The front wheels
were pointing in different directions, only one of them responding to
steering inputs..

He did this at the narrowest part of the access road, right outside my
folks' front door. And I wanted to go home.

I produced a trolley jack to swing the back end round, then the
combined (limited) might of 8 slightly merry youths from the party
sufficed to push/drag/slide the car out of the way.

He creased both fright doors and both right wings of the Golf, and
broke the steering. I am thinking his dad will not be best pleased,
that sounds like a write-off to me.

Easily done though, in the dark. Locals know that the post is there
(and it is white, and has a reflector). The owner put it there to
protect his dry-stone wall. Not without good cause.
