RE: Free electric car charging ports

Posted by M Hodson <markhodson.com-at-gmail.com> on 31 January 2014 | 0 Comments

>Or introduce a standard battery pack that can be interchanged between all
vehicles.
>There is the physical side to consider, but it could be done, that way,
you pull up at a service station, >
>pay for a set of charged batteries, somebody/something changes them for
you and off you go again

1) The batteries hold less charge as they age (and depending on use) so
your swapped out battery might only be 50% of the one you started with.
2) Unless we standardise on using a whole bunch of AA eneloops, the
batteries affect the design of the vehicle (ride height, boot shape, etc.)
so the batteries would be compromised to achieve standardisation.
3) Dreamline battery packs

Nope - the only way to do it is to liquidise the batteries in some way, and
pour the energy into holding tanks in the vehicles.
I think we need pumps dispensing finely powdered lead and high molar
suphuric acid.
